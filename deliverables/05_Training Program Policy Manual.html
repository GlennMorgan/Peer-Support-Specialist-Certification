Rhombus University
Training Program Policy Manual

Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
80-Hour Core Training

This policy manual defines institutional expectations governing admission, attendance, conduct, grading, completion, accommodations, confidentiality, grievance procedures, and administrative standards for the Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Program.

Program Purpose

The Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Program prepares learners for professional peer support roles within behavioral health environments aligned with Medi-Cal service delivery expectations. This manual establishes policies that ensure instructional quality, fairness, learner support, and compliance with workforce training standards.

Admissions Policy

Eligibility Requirements

Applicants must meet the following criteria:
  • Be at least 18 years of age
  • Demonstrate lived experience relevant to peer support services
  • Be willing to participate in a structured learning environment
  • Demonstrate readiness for workforce-aligned training
  • Complete application documentation

Recommended Readiness Indicators

Applicants are encouraged to demonstrate:
  • Commitment to recovery-oriented principles
  • Ability to participate respectfully in group settings
  • Stability sufficient to complete structured coursework
  • Interest in behavioral health service environments

Enrollment Procedures

Applicants must complete:
  • Program application form
  • Eligibility screening interview when applicable
  • Orientation session participation
  • Technology readiness confirmation for online delivery

Attendance Policy

Minimum Attendance Requirement

Learners must complete the full 80 instructional hours. Absences exceeding:
  • 8 total hours
may result in ineligibility for course completion unless remediation requirements are satisfied.

Late Arrival Policy

Repeated late arrival may be recorded as partial absence. Three late arrivals equal one absence hour.

Excused Absences

Excused absences may include:
  • Medical necessity
  • Family emergency
  • Documented hardship circumstances
Instructor approval is required for make-up eligibility.

Make-Up Instruction Policy

Learners missing instructional time may complete:
  • Assigned supplemental coursework
  • Recorded lecture review
  • Instructor consultation sessions
  • Skills demonstration replacement exercises
Make-up instruction must be completed before course completion certification is issued.

Participation Expectations

Learners are expected to:
  • Attend scheduled sessions
  • Participate respectfully
  • Complete assignments
  • Engage in group exercises
  • Maintain confidentiality within training discussions

Professional Conduct Policy

Learners must demonstrate behavior consistent with peer workforce expectations. Professional conduct includes:
  • Respectful communication
  • Appropriate boundaries
  • Constructive participation
  • Cultural humility
  • Responsible use of lived experience
Disruptive conduct may result in corrective action.

Code of Ethics Alignment

Training participants are expected to demonstrate alignment with core peer support ethics:
  • Hope-centered engagement
  • Person-driven services
  • Confidentiality protection
  • Respect for autonomy
  • Non-coercive support practices

Confidentiality Policy

Learners must protect the privacy of:
  • Class participants
  • Shared lived experience stories
  • Case examples
  • Practice scenarios involving identifiable individuals
Disclosure of confidential information outside the training environment is prohibited.

Academic Integrity Policy

Learners must complete all coursework independently unless collaborative activities are assigned. Academic integrity violations include:
  • Submitting another participant’s work
  • Misrepresenting attendance
  • Providing false documentation
  • Altering evaluation records
Violations may result in removal from the program.

Evaluation and Grading Policy

Learners are evaluated using:
Evaluation Component Description
Knowledge Checks Short quizzes verifying module understanding
Skills Demonstrations Observed peer support practice exercises
Documentation Exercises Practice progress note completion
Participation Review Professional engagement and conduct assessment
Final Assessment Comprehensive competency evaluation

Completion Requirements

To receive a Certificate of Completion, learners must:
  • Attend required instructional hours
  • Complete assignments
  • Demonstrate competency in skills practice
  • Pass final assessment requirements
  • Meet professionalism expectations

Remediation Policy

Learners who do not meet completion requirements may qualify for remediation. Remediation options include:
  • Assignment resubmission
  • Additional skills demonstrations
  • Instructor consultation sessions
  • Supplemental learning activities
Remediation timelines are determined by the instructional team.

Withdrawal Policy

Learners may withdraw voluntarily before course completion. Withdrawal procedures include:
  • Written notification submission
  • Exit consultation when available
  • Documentation of hours completed

Dismissal Policy

Learners may be dismissed for:
  • Repeated absence
  • Disruptive conduct
  • Violation of confidentiality
  • Academic dishonesty
  • Safety concerns affecting the learning environment

Grievance Procedure

Learners may submit grievances regarding:
  • Instructional concerns
  • Program policies
  • Evaluation decisions
  • Discrimination concerns
Grievance process steps:
  1. Submit written concern
  2. Program review within reasonable timeframe
  3. Formal response issued
  4. Appeal opportunity if needed

Accessibility and Accommodation Policy

Rhombus University provides reasonable accommodations consistent with applicable accessibility standards. Examples include:
  • Extended assessment time
  • Accessible instructional materials
  • Alternative participation formats
  • Technology support accommodations
Accommodation requests should be submitted prior to course start whenever possible.

Nondiscrimination Policy

Rhombus University does not discriminate based on:
  • Race
  • Ethnicity
  • Religion
  • Gender identity
  • Sexual orientation
  • Disability status
  • Age
  • National origin
  • Recovery background

Technology Use Policy

Learners participating in online instruction must:
  • Maintain reliable internet access
  • Participate using identifiable learner credentials
  • Protect login confidentiality
  • Use technology respectfully
Recording sessions without permission is prohibited.

Record Retention Policy

Rhombus University maintains training records including:
  • Attendance logs
  • Assessment outcomes
  • Completion verification
  • Instructor evaluations
  • Certificate issuance documentation
Records are retained consistent with institutional training documentation standards.

Certificate Issuance Policy

Certificates are issued after verification that:
  • All hours are completed
  • Assignments are submitted
  • Final evaluation requirements are met
  • Professional conduct expectations are satisfied

Program Modification Policy

Rhombus University reserves the right to adjust:
  • course schedule
  • delivery format
  • instructional sequencing
  • assignment structure
while maintaining required instructional hours and competency coverage.

Learner Support Policy

Learners may request support through:
  • Instructor consultation
  • Program coordinator assistance
  • Technology support guidance
  • Academic remediation planning

Policy Acknowledgment Requirement

Learners must acknowledge review of this policy manual prior to beginning instruction. Documentation of acknowledgment is maintained within learner training records.
