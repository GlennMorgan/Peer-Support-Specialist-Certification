Institution Mission

Rhombus University provides structured, recovery-informed workforce education designed to prepare individuals with lived experience for meaningful roles within behavioral health systems. The institution emphasizes dignity, person-driven engagement, ethical service delivery, and practical readiness for peer support employment environments.

Programs are designed to strengthen access to peer workforce pathways while supporting community-based service organizations seeking trained peer support professionals aligned with Medi-Cal expectations.

Training Program Description

The Rhombus University Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program is an 80-hour competency-based curriculum preparing participants to serve in peer support roles across behavioral health, recovery services, housing navigation environments, crisis-support settings, and community-based care coordination programs.

Instruction emphasizes recovery principles, advocacy, documentation readiness, cultural humility, trauma-informed engagement, communication skills, confidentiality standards, and safety awareness consistent with the professional peer support role.

Instructor Qualifications Framework

Rhombus University instructors include certified peer specialists, behavioral health professionals, and subject matter experts with experience in recovery-oriented service delivery and adult workforce training environments.

Instructor teams demonstrate:
  • peer workforce experience
  • lived-experience-informed engagement practice
  • behavioral health systems familiarity
  • adult-learning facilitation capability
  • cultural humility and trauma-informed training delivery

Target Learner Population

The program serves individuals who:
  • have lived experience related to recovery or behavioral health challenges
  • seek entry into peer-support workforce roles
  • are preparing for certification eligibility pathways
  • are transitioning from recovery-support environments into employment
  • are referred by community-based behavioral health providers

Workforce Alignment

The Peer Support Specialist Training Program supports workforce readiness for roles within:

  • county behavioral health programs
  • community-based recovery organizations
  • housing stabilization programs
  • care coordination environments
  • crisis-support systems
  • navigation and referral services

Training emphasizes practical readiness for participation within interdisciplinary behavioral health teams.

Community and System Alignment

Rhombus University training is designed to align with the needs of California behavioral health service providers seeking trained peer-support personnel capable of supporting recovery-oriented service delivery models consistent with Medi-Cal program expectations.

Instruction prepares participants to collaborate effectively within structured service environments while maintaining appropriate peer role boundaries.
