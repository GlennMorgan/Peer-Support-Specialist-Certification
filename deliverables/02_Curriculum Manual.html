Curriculum Manual, 80-Hour Core Training Program
Program Purpose
This curriculum manual presents the Rhombus University 80-hour Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program. The program is designed to prepare trainees for ethical, recovery-oriented, person-driven peer support practice in California behavioral health settings. The course is structured to support state-aligned peer certification preparation, workforce readiness, and practical field application.

Program Overview

Program Title Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training
Institution Rhombus University
Total Hours 80 instructional hours
Delivery Model Online-first, live instruction with guided practice, skills labs, reading, reflection, and applied activities
Audience Individuals pursuing California Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist training and workforce preparation
Course Outcome Certificate of Completion for the 80-hour core training, upon successful completion of attendance, assessment, and professionalism requirements

Program Philosophy

Rhombus University delivers this program through a practical, structured, and student-supportive model that emphasizes hope, recovery, wellness, dignity, personal responsibility, cultural humility, ethical conduct, trauma-informed practice, and workforce readiness.

The training is grounded in the professional role of the peer support specialist. It is not clinical training. It does not authorize diagnosis, psychotherapy, prescribing, or other licensed behavioral health services. Instead, it prepares trainees to use lived experience appropriately and professionally in service of others’ recovery and resilience.

Program Learning Outcomes

By the end of the 80-hour program, trainees will be able to:

  1. Define the role, purpose, and scope of the Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist.
  2. Apply recovery-oriented, person-driven, and culturally responsive peer practices.
  3. Use ethical self-disclosure, active listening, advocacy, and engagement skills appropriately.
  4. Support navigation, warm handoffs, community inclusion, and recovery planning.
  5. Demonstrate appropriate boundaries, confidentiality practices, and documentation standards.
  6. Recognize crisis indicators, respond within peer scope, and activate proper support pathways.
  7. Demonstrate professionalism, self-awareness, self-care, and readiness for peer employment settings.

Detailed Module Descriptions

Module 1, Foundations of Peer Support and Recovery, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module establishes the foundation of professional peer support practice, including the peer role, recovery and wellness orientation, ethics, scope of practice, and the California certification pathway.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose and role of peer support in behavioral health settings.
  • Distinguish peer practice from clinical practice.
  • Describe hope, recovery, wellness, and resilience using person-driven language.
  • Understand the professional use of lived experience.

Topics Covered

  • Peer movement and history
  • Recovery, resiliency, and wellness principles
  • Role clarity and scope of practice
  • Professional identity and ethics
  • Purposeful self-disclosure

Instructional Methods

Interactive lecture, guided discussion, reflective writing, scope sorting exercise, and recovery-language practice.

Module 2, Advocacy, Rights, Consumers, and Families, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module trains students to support self-determination, reduce stigma, strengthen voice, and promote effective advocacy with individuals and families in behavioral health systems.

Learning Objectives

  • Differentiate self-advocacy from system advocacy.
  • Support individuals in expressing needs and choices.
  • Recognize the role of families and caregivers while protecting autonomy.
  • Identify stigma and empowerment barriers.

Topics Covered

  • Rights and access issues
  • Empowerment and self-determination
  • Consumer and family voice
  • Stigma reduction
  • Advocacy language and coaching techniques

Instructional Methods

Mini-lecture, scenario analysis, role-play, and advocacy skill practice.

Module 3, Behavioral Health Foundations and Co-Occurring Conditions, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module provides non-clinical behavioral health literacy so peer specialists can work effectively within systems, support recovery, and maintain clear scope boundaries.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe broad categories of mental health and substance use challenges.
  • Explain co-occurring conditions and multiple recovery pathways.
  • Identify peer-appropriate responses and referral needs.
  • Support informed choice without acting as a clinician.

Topics Covered

  • Mental health overview
  • Substance use overview
  • Co-occurring conditions
  • Harm reduction and recovery pathways
  • Medication support boundaries

Instructional Methods

Lecture, case review, facilitated Q&A, and recovery-pathway comparison exercises.

Module 4, Cultural Humility, Structural Competence, and Trauma-Informed Practice, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module strengthens the trainee’s ability to serve with respect, responsiveness, inclusion, and awareness of trauma, identity, access barriers, and structural inequities.

Learning Objectives

  • Practice cultural humility as an ongoing discipline.
  • Recognize structural barriers affecting care and recovery.
  • Apply trauma-informed engagement principles.
  • Use inclusive, non-coercive, responsive communication.

Topics Covered

  • Bias awareness and cultural humility
  • Structural barriers and inequity
  • Trauma-informed principles
  • Disability, language, identity, and access considerations
  • Practical adaptations in peer interactions

Instructional Methods

Facilitated reflection, guided case consultation, communication drills, and scenario repair practice.

Module 5, Communication, Groups, Conflict Resolution, and Boundaries, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module develops the interpersonal skill set needed for peer engagement, group support, conflict response, emotional regulation support, and safe professional boundaries.

Learning Objectives

  • Use active listening and reflective responding.
  • Demonstrate appropriate motivational and encouragement strategies.
  • Facilitate group participation and shared discussion.
  • Respond to conflict and de-escalate within peer scope.
  • Maintain boundaries and appropriate self-disclosure.

Topics Covered

  • Listening and engagement micro-skills
  • Motivational support language
  • Group facilitation basics
  • Conflict response and de-escalation
  • Boundary management and red flags

Instructional Methods

Live demonstration, fishbowl facilitation, role-play, and structured peer feedback.

Module 6, Recovery Planning, Self-Awareness, and Self-Care, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module teaches trainees to support strengths-based recovery planning while also building their own sustainable self-care and professional resilience practices.

Learning Objectives

  • Support person-driven recovery goals and action steps.
  • Identify strengths, barriers, and wellness tools.
  • Recognize the relationship between self-awareness and service quality.
  • Create a personal self-care and support plan.

Topics Covered

  • Strengths-based planning
  • Goal setting and recovery support
  • Coping, resilience, and wellness tools
  • Burnout prevention
  • Self-awareness and self-care planning

Instructional Methods

Worksheet lab, guided coaching exercises, reflection, and self-care planning sessions.

Module 7, Community Inclusion, Navigation, and Employment Readiness, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module equips trainees to connect people to community resources, support inclusion, make warm handoffs, and prepare for entry into peer support employment.

Learning Objectives

  • Perform community resource navigation and warm handoffs.
  • Promote social connection and community participation.
  • Support employment readiness and workplace professionalism.
  • Strengthen practical readiness for peer support roles.

Topics Covered

  • Community mapping
  • Housing, benefits, and service navigation
  • Natural supports and inclusion planning
  • Warm handoff process
  • Résumé, interview, and certification readiness

Instructional Methods

Resource mapping lab, referral exercises, mock interview practice, and employment-readiness coaching.

Module 8, Documentation, Confidentiality, Digital Literacy, and Team Practice, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module prepares trainees to document peer services appropriately, protect confidentiality, use technology responsibly, and function effectively on multidisciplinary teams.

Learning Objectives

  • Write clear, accurate, and role-appropriate progress notes.
  • Protect confidential information in written, verbal, and digital communication.
  • Use digital systems and communication tools professionally.
  • Coordinate appropriately with team members.

Topics Covered

  • Documentation basics
  • Objective language and service note structure
  • Confidentiality limits and privacy practices
  • Digital literacy and professional communication
  • Team coordination and role clarity

Instructional Methods

Documentation lab, note correction exercises, confidentiality scenarios, and team communication simulation.

Module 9, Safety, Crisis Planning, and Mandated Responsibilities, 8 Hours

Purpose

This module prepares trainees to recognize crisis indicators, respond safely within peer scope, support safety planning, and follow required reporting and escalation procedures.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify crisis warning signs and urgency indicators.
  • Participate in safety and crisis planning.
  • Recognize scope limits and when immediate escalation is required.
  • Understand mandated responsibilities and reporting pathways.

Topics Covered

  • Crisis spectrum and peer role
  • Safety planning basics
  • Emergency and support pathways
  • Mandatory reporting awareness
  • Debrief and consultation practices

Instructional Methods

Simulation, protocol review, decision-tree exercises, and structured debrief.

Module 10, Integration, Skills Demonstration, Exam Preparation, and Transition to Work, 8 Hours

Purpose

This final module integrates all core competencies, measures skill readiness, prepares trainees for certification-related next steps, and supports transition into workforce environments.

Learning Objectives

  • Demonstrate integrated peer support skills in applied scenarios.
  • Complete final written and practical evaluations.
  • Understand the next steps toward certification and employment.
  • Create an individual professional development and transition plan.

Topics Covered

  • Comprehensive review
  • Skills demonstration stations
  • Final written assessment
  • Professional transition planning
  • Optional practicum orientation

Instructional Methods

Mock exam, skills demonstration, coaching, review sessions, and transition planning.
