Rhombus University

Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program

80-hour core training designed to prepare learners for professional peer support roles in recovery-oriented, behavioral health, navigation, and community support environments.

Program Overview

Rhombus University offers a structured, workforce-focused Peer Support Specialist training program that equips participants with the core knowledge, communication skills, ethical framework, and practical readiness needed for peer support work in California service environments.

What Participants Will Learn

  • Foundations of peer support and recovery
  • Advocacy, rights, and empowerment
  • Behavioral health and co-occurring condition basics
  • Cultural humility and trauma-informed practice
  • Communication, boundaries, and conflict resolution
  • Recovery planning and self-care
  • Navigation, referrals, and community linkage
  • Documentation, confidentiality, and team practice
  • Safety awareness and crisis response within role scope
  • Workforce readiness and final competency preparation

Who This Program Is For

  • Individuals with lived experience relevant to peer support work
  • People preparing for peer workforce opportunities
  • Community members seeking structured recovery-support training
  • Organizations building peer-support staffing pipelines
  • Participants transitioning toward service-oriented helping roles

Program Details

Institution Rhombus University
Program Title Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
Length 80 instructional hours
Format Online-first, with hybrid and in-person delivery options as available
Completion Certificate of Completion and official transcript issued upon successful completion
Instruction Style Lecture, skills labs, guided discussion, scenario-based learning, documentation practice, and final competency review

Program Strengths

  • Recovery-oriented and person-centered
  • Practical and workforce-focused
  • Structured professional expectations
  • Ethics and boundaries emphasized throughout
  • Built for behavioral health and community support settings

Optional Next Step

Eligible learners may also pursue the optional Peer Workforce Readiness Practicum, a supervised practicum overlay designed to strengthen applied readiness for peer support roles.
