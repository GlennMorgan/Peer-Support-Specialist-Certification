Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
80-Hour Core Training Curriculum

This handbook provides guidance for participants enrolled in the Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Program. It explains program expectations, policies, participation requirements, and completion standards.

Welcome to Rhombus University

Welcome to the Peer Support Specialist Training Program. This course prepares learners with lived experience to enter recovery-oriented support roles within behavioral health, housing navigation, community services, and interdisciplinary service teams.

Participants are expected to engage actively, maintain professional conduct, and complete required instructional and assessment activities.

Program Overview

Program Element Description
Program Title Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
Total Hours 80 instructional hours
Delivery Format Online, hybrid, or in-person
Instruction Methods Lecture, guided discussion, skills practice, scenario exercises, reflection, and competency review
Completion Award Certificate of Completion and official transcript

Program Learning Goals

Participants will learn to:
  • apply recovery-oriented peer support principles
  • demonstrate respectful communication skills
  • maintain appropriate professional boundaries
  • support advocacy and self-determination
  • assist with service navigation and referrals
  • understand confidentiality responsibilities
  • participate effectively within service teams
  • recognize safety and escalation situations

Participation Expectations

Students are expected to:
  • attend all scheduled training sessions
  • arrive on time and remain present during instruction
  • participate respectfully in discussions and exercises
  • complete assigned learning activities
  • demonstrate professional communication
  • follow instructor guidance and course policies

Attendance Policy

Participants must complete the full instructional hour requirement to receive a certificate of completion.

Requirement Description
Total Required Hours 80 hours
Late Arrival May result in missed-hour tracking
Early Departure May reduce verified attendance hours
Absence Makeup May be permitted depending on instructor approval

Professional Conduct Expectations

Students must:
  • treat instructors and classmates respectfully
  • maintain confidentiality within discussions
  • avoid disruptive behavior
  • participate in good faith learning activities
  • demonstrate readiness for peer workforce roles

Confidentiality Guidelines

Training discussions may include sensitive topics. Participants are expected to maintain confidentiality regarding personal information shared by others during the course.

Participants should not:
  • share personal stories from classmates outside training
  • record sessions without permission
  • disclose identifying participant information

Technology Expectations (Online or Hybrid Cohorts)

Students participating remotely should:
  • log in using their assigned identity
  • maintain a stable internet connection when possible
  • participate using camera when requested
  • avoid distractions during instruction
  • communicate technical challenges promptly

Assignments and Learning Activities

Students may be asked to complete:
  • reflection exercises
  • scenario-based participation
  • communication practice activities
  • documentation awareness exercises
  • group discussion participation
  • knowledge checks

Assessment Requirements

Completion requires participation in:
Assessment Type Description
Knowledge Checks Short learning verification activities
Skills Demonstrations Observed peer-support communication exercises
Scenario Participation Applied learning activities
Final Competency Review End-of-course readiness evaluation

Code of Ethical Learning Conduct

Participants agree to:
  • respect diverse experiences and perspectives
  • engage in person-centered dialogue
  • avoid judgmental language
  • maintain appropriate peer-role boundaries
  • support a trauma-informed learning environment

Safety and Support

Training may include discussion of recovery-related experiences. Students are encouraged to communicate with instructors if support is needed during participation.

Instructors may:
  • pause activities if needed
  • offer grounding exercises
  • provide resource referrals
  • adjust participation expectations when appropriate

Certificate Eligibility Requirements

To receive a certificate, students must:
  • complete required instructional hours
  • participate in required learning activities
  • demonstrate professional conduct
  • complete competency review requirements

Optional Practicum Opportunity

Eligible graduates may participate in the optional:

Peer Workforce Readiness Practicum Overlay

This practicum provides supervised exposure to peer-support service environments following completion of the training program.

Student Feedback Opportunities

Students are encouraged to provide feedback through:
  • course evaluation forms
  • instructor feedback sessions
  • program improvement surveys

Equal Participation Commitment

Rhombus University supports a respectful learning environment that welcomes participants from diverse backgrounds and lived experiences. Training promotes:
  • cultural humility
  • equity-minded engagement
  • recovery-oriented dialogue
  • inclusive participation
