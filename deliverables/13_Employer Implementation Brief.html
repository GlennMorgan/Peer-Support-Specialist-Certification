Employer Implementation Brief

Integrating Peer Support Specialists Trained Through the Rhombus University Program

This implementation brief is designed for behavioral health agencies, recovery organizations, housing programs, navigation services, and community-based providers preparing to integrate Peer Support Specialists trained through the Rhombus University 80-hour Medi-Cal-aligned training program.

Purpose of This Document

The purpose of this brief is to support employers in successfully onboarding and integrating trained Peer Support Specialists into service teams. The document outlines role expectations, supervision structures, scope considerations, documentation readiness, and workforce alignment strategies.

This guidance helps agencies translate training completion into effective peer workforce deployment.

Overview of the Rhombus University Training Program

Program Element Description
Program Name Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
Total Instructional Hours 80 hours
Instructional Model Competency-based workforce preparation
Primary Focus Areas Recovery principles, communication, advocacy, documentation awareness, ethics, navigation skills, and team collaboration
Delivery Format Online, hybrid, and in-person options

Peer Support Role Overview

Peer Support Specialists bring lived experience and recovery-informed engagement skills into structured service environments. Their role complements clinical care by strengthening trust, engagement, and continuity of support.

Peer specialists commonly support:
  • engagement and rapport-building
  • resource navigation assistance
  • recovery goal support
  • service participation encouragement
  • group facilitation assistance
  • system navigation guidance
  • community linkage support
  • self-advocacy development

Typical Service Settings

Peer Support Specialists may serve within:
  • county behavioral health programs
  • outpatient treatment environments
  • housing stabilization programs
  • recovery community organizations
  • care coordination teams
  • warm line and navigation services
  • substance use recovery programs
  • peer drop-in centers

Competencies Developed Through Training

Graduates of the Rhombus University training program demonstrate preparation in:
  • recovery-oriented communication practices
  • person-centered engagement techniques
  • professional boundaries
  • cultural humility
  • trauma-informed interaction
  • documentation awareness
  • ethical role conduct
  • team collaboration readiness
  • resource navigation fundamentals
  • safety awareness and escalation pathways

Recommended Employer Onboarding Steps

Employers integrating Peer Support Specialists should:
  • define peer role scope within the organization
  • assign a qualified supervisor
  • provide documentation training aligned with agency workflow
  • clarify escalation procedures
  • introduce confidentiality policies
  • review mandated reporting responsibilities
  • establish supervision frequency
  • outline interdisciplinary team expectations

Supervision Structure Recommendations

Supervision Element Description
Supervisor Qualification Behavioral health professional or certified peer supervisor, depending on agency structure
Supervision Frequency Weekly during initial employment phase recommended
Role Clarification Ongoing reinforcement of peer role boundaries and responsibilities
Documentation Review Routine review for accuracy and compliance with agency standards
Professional Development Encouragement of continued peer workforce education

Scope of Practice Considerations

Peer Support Specialists typically:
  • share recovery-informed perspective appropriately
  • support service engagement
  • assist with navigation of services
  • encourage goal-setting participation
  • facilitate recovery conversations
Peer Support Specialists do not:
  • provide clinical diagnosis
  • deliver psychotherapy
  • conduct formal risk assessments independently
  • determine treatment plans
  • operate outside supervision structures

Documentation Readiness

Training includes exposure to:
  • confidentiality standards
  • professional note structure awareness
  • service interaction summaries
  • role-appropriate documentation language
  • team communication expectations
Employers should provide agency-specific documentation templates during onboarding.

Integration into Interdisciplinary Teams

Peer Support Specialists strengthen team effectiveness by:
  • providing lived-experience perspective
  • supporting engagement continuity
  • bridging communication gaps
  • reinforcing recovery goals
  • supporting culturally responsive service delivery

Workplace Environment Recommendations

Successful peer integration is supported when agencies:
  • define clear peer role expectations
  • maintain respectful team collaboration culture
  • provide supervision access
  • encourage peer voice in care discussions
  • avoid assigning clinical responsibilities outside role scope

Employment Pathway Alignment

Graduates of the Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Program may be prepared for roles including:
  • Peer Support Specialist
  • Recovery Support Specialist
  • Navigation Support Worker
  • Engagement Specialist
  • Housing Support Peer
  • Community Linkage Peer
  • Warm Line Support Staff

Continuing Workforce Development

Employers are encouraged to support ongoing development through:
  • advanced peer-support coursework
  • supervisor-guided skill expansion
  • documentation refinement training
  • team-based learning opportunities
  • participation in recovery-oriented service initiatives

Verification of Training Completion

Employers may confirm training completion through:
  • Rhombus University Certificate of Completion
  • Official Transcript of Instructional Hours
  • Program verification contact (upon request)
