Instructor Qualification Packet

Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program, 80-Hour Core Training
Purpose of this Packet
This Instructor Qualification Packet documents the instructional staffing structure, minimum qualification standards, faculty role expectations, module assignment framework, required submission components, and instructor attestation language for the Rhombus University Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program.

Program Instructional Staffing Model

Rhombus University will deliver the Peer Support Specialist Training Program through a structured instructional team designed to provide subject matter expertise, lived-experience credibility, adult-learning competence, and workforce relevance. The staffing model supports both instructional quality and compliance readiness.

Instructional Role Primary Function Minimum Qualification Standard Program Responsibility
Lead Instructor Primary faculty lead for core curriculum delivery Current Certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist, or licensed / registered California behavioral health professional with adult training experience Course delivery oversight, instructional consistency, learner performance review, final instructional sign-off
Peer Co-Instructor Co-facilitates peer-specific content and skills labs Current Certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist with direct peer service experience and facilitation ability Lived-experience integration, role modeling, skills coaching, peer practice lab facilitation
Adjunct Subject Matter Instructor Delivers specialized content areas Demonstrated subject matter competence in assigned content area Documentation, confidentiality, cultural humility, crisis, employment readiness, or related specialized training segments

Instructor Qualification Standards

1. Lead Instructor Standard

  • Must demonstrate strong knowledge of peer support practice and recovery-oriented service delivery.
  • Should hold current California peer certification or qualifying behavioral health professional credentials.
  • Must demonstrate experience teaching adults in structured instructional settings.
  • Must understand role boundaries between peer services and clinical services.
  • Should demonstrate knowledge of California behavioral health systems and workforce expectations.

2. Peer Co-Instructor Standard

  • Must demonstrate directly relevant lived experience appropriate to peer workforce training.
  • Should have a minimum of two years of peer service or peer leadership experience, where possible.
  • Must demonstrate the ability to use lived experience professionally and appropriately in instructional settings.
  • Must support learner skill development in self-disclosure, advocacy, recovery language, and ethical peer engagement.

3. Adjunct Instructor Standard

  • Must possess relevant subject matter expertise for assigned module content.
  • Should demonstrate training, facilitation, or public instruction ability.
  • Must agree to teach within Rhombus University curriculum structure and instructional standards.
  • Must support person-driven, non-coercive, culturally responsive, and trauma-informed educational delivery.

4. Faculty Composition Standard

  • The instructional team should include meaningful lived-experience representation.
  • At least one primary instructor or co-instructor should demonstrate direct peer workforce experience.
  • The faculty mix should support both credibility in peer practice and professionalism in public behavioral health training environments.

Instructor Responsibilities

  • Teach assigned content in accordance with the approved Rhombus University curriculum.
  • Maintain instructional professionalism and consistency across cohorts.
  • Use person-driven, respectful, and non-coercive teaching practices.
  • Protect confidentiality and ensure that class discussions avoid inappropriate disclosure of protected information.
  • Support an inclusive learning environment across race, ethnicity, language, religion, disability, gender identity, sexual orientation, and lived-experience background.
  • Deliver content in a trauma-informed manner that promotes safety, trust, and learner dignity.
  • Participate in student evaluation and scoring processes as assigned.
  • Communicate concerns related to learner conduct, safety, or instructional integrity through designated Rhombus University channels.

Instructional Integrity Standards

All instructors must teach within the approved course structure and may not substitute unrelated content, personal ideology, clinical role assumptions, or non-approved course objectives in place of required peer support curriculum. Faculty are expected to preserve the integrity of the training program while maintaining appropriate flexibility in facilitation style, examples, and learner support.
