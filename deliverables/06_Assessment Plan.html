Assessment Plan

Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program
80-Hour Core Training Curriculum

This assessment plan describes how Rhombus University evaluates learner knowledge, applied skills, professional readiness, and competency development across the Peer Support Specialist Training Program. The evaluation structure supports workforce readiness expectations and certification-aligned competency verification.

Assessment Philosophy

Assessment within the Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Program is competency-based and practice-oriented. Learners demonstrate mastery through knowledge checks, applied peer-support skills demonstrations, documentation exercises, participation standards, and a final competency review.

Evaluation methods emphasize recovery-oriented communication, ethical practice, role clarity, cultural responsiveness, and professional conduct rather than academic theory alone.

Assessment Objectives

Learners are evaluated to confirm their ability to:
  • Understand the role and scope of peer support specialists
  • Apply recovery-oriented engagement strategies
  • Demonstrate ethical boundaries and confidentiality awareness
  • Support advocacy and navigation activities
  • Use trauma-informed and culturally responsive practices
  • Recognize crisis indicators and respond appropriately within scope
  • Document peer services using role-appropriate language
  • Demonstrate readiness for peer workforce environments

Knowledge Check Assessments

Knowledge checks are brief structured evaluations designed to confirm learner understanding of:
  • peer support scope of practice
  • recovery principles
  • cultural humility expectations
  • confidentiality requirements
  • documentation standards
  • crisis response boundaries
Knowledge checks may include:
  • multiple-choice questions
  • short written responses
  • scenario interpretation prompts
Passing threshold: 70 percent or higher.

Skills Demonstration Assessments

Skills demonstrations evaluate applied peer-support competencies through structured practice scenarios. Learners demonstrate ability to:
  • use recovery-oriented language
  • apply active listening techniques
  • maintain boundaries appropriately
  • support strengths-based goal discussion
  • respond to advocacy needs
  • recognize escalation indicators
Skills demonstrations are evaluated using standardized instructor observation rubrics.

Documentation Skills Evaluation

Learners complete structured documentation exercises aligned with peer-support service expectations. Evaluation criteria include:
  • clarity
  • objectivity
  • scope-appropriate language
  • avoidance of clinical terminology outside role boundaries
  • accurate activity description
  • confidentiality compliance

Participation and Professional Conduct Evaluation

Participation is evaluated throughout the training program. Criteria include:
  • respectful engagement
  • group collaboration
  • confidentiality adherence
  • responsible use of lived experience
  • consistent attendance
  • completion of assignments
Professional conduct concerns may result in remediation requirements.

Final Competency Assessment

The final competency assessment includes:
Component Description
Written Knowledge Review Measures understanding of core peer-support concepts
Applied Scenario Exercise Evaluates communication and ethical decision-making
Documentation Demonstration Confirms role-appropriate service-note writing ability
Professional Readiness Review Instructor evaluation of workforce readiness indicators

Assessment Scoring Framework

Evaluation Area Passing Standard
Knowledge Checks Minimum 70 percent
Skills Demonstrations Meets competency rubric expectations
Documentation Exercises Accurate and scope-aligned submission
Participation Satisfactory professional engagement
Final Assessment Overall competency demonstrated

Instructor Evaluation Rubric Framework

Skills demonstration performance is evaluated using the following indicators:
  • active listening
  • respectful engagement
  • recovery-oriented language
  • appropriate boundaries
  • confidentiality awareness
  • cultural responsiveness
  • accurate referral awareness
  • scope adherence

Remediation Policy

Learners who do not meet assessment expectations may complete remediation activities such as:
  • assignment revision
  • additional documentation practice
  • supplemental knowledge review
  • repeat skills demonstration
  • instructor consultation session
Remediation must be completed prior to certificate issuance eligibility.

Reassessment Policy

Learners may repeat:
  • knowledge checks
  • documentation exercises
  • skills demonstrations
after instructor consultation and corrective feedback.

Attendance and Assessment Integration

Completion eligibility requires:
  • minimum instructional hour attendance
  • successful assessment participation
  • completion of required assignments
  • professional conduct compliance

Instructor Documentation Responsibilities

Instructional staff maintain assessment records including:
  • quiz outcomes
  • skills demonstration scoring
  • documentation exercise review
  • attendance verification
  • final competency determination

Completion Determination Criteria

Learners receive a Certificate of Completion when they:
  • complete all required instructional hours
  • meet minimum knowledge thresholds
  • demonstrate applied peer-support skills
  • submit acceptable documentation exercises
  • maintain professional conduct standards
  • successfully complete the final competency assessment

Assessment Record Retention

Rhombus University maintains assessment documentation including:
  • grading records
  • skills demonstration rubrics
  • attendance verification
  • completion determinations
Records are retained according to institutional training documentation standards.
