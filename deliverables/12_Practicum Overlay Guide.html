Peer Workforce Readiness Practicum
Supplemental Practicum Overlay for the Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Training Program

This practicum overlay is an optional workforce-readiness component offered in addition to the 80-hour core training curriculum. It is designed to strengthen learner readiness for entry-level peer support environments through supervised observation, structured practice, reflection, and professional feedback.

Purpose

The Rhombus University Peer Workforce Readiness Practicum is designed to provide learners with structured exposure to real-world peer service environments following successful completion of the core training curriculum. The practicum supports transition from classroom-based learning into supervised workforce preparation.

This practicum is intended as an educational and workforce-readiness enhancement. It is not a substitute for employer onboarding, licensure, certification examination, or direct service authorization beyond the learner’s approved role.

Practicum Goals

The practicum is intended to help learners:

  • observe the peer support role in real service environments
  • apply recovery-oriented communication skills
  • practice professional boundaries in supervised settings
  • strengthen resource navigation and referral readiness
  • develop workplace confidence and professional conduct habits
  • understand team roles, documentation expectations, and escalation pathways

Eligibility Requirements

To begin the practicum overlay, the learner must:

  • complete the 80-hour Rhombus University core training
  • meet attendance and completion standards
  • demonstrate satisfactory professional conduct during training
  • complete practicum orientation requirements
  • submit required practicum placement documents

Recommended Practicum Site Types

Appropriate practicum host sites may include:

  • behavioral health outpatient programs
  • recovery community organizations
  • housing navigation or stabilization programs
  • peer drop-in centers
  • warm line or support line environments
  • substance use recovery service settings
  • family support programs
  • community-based care coordination programs

Site Qualification Standards

Practicum sites should be able to provide:

  • a clearly identified site supervisor or preceptor
  • orientation to site expectations and confidentiality rules
  • a safe and appropriate environment for observation and supervised learning
  • structured opportunities for limited peer-support role practice
  • midpoint and final learner feedback

Permitted Learner Activities

Learners may participate in supervised activities such as:

  • observing peer support interactions
  • participating in group support settings under supervision
  • assisting with resource navigation tasks
  • practicing warm handoff communication
  • reviewing workflows and documentation expectations
  • completing structured reflection exercises
  • participating in supervised outreach or engagement tasks, where approved

Restricted Learner Activities

Learners may not:

  • provide independent billable services
  • represent themselves as licensed clinicians
  • perform unsupervised crisis intervention beyond role and site policy
  • complete official documentation without required oversight
  • access confidential information beyond authorized learning need
  • perform duties outside approved practicum scope

Supervision Structure

Supervision Role Responsibility
Site Preceptor Provides day-to-day supervision, site orientation, observation, and performance feedback
Rhombus Faculty Liaison Monitors educational alignment, reviews learner reflections, supports issue resolution, and verifies completion requirements
Learner Maintains professional conduct, completes assigned hours, participates in supervision, and submits required practicum documents

Practicum Learning Objectives

By the end of the practicum overlay, the learner should be able to:

  • describe the peer support role as observed within a service environment
  • demonstrate respectful, person-driven communication
  • maintain appropriate professional boundaries
  • identify when referral or escalation is needed
  • support basic navigation and community linkage tasks
  • demonstrate workplace professionalism and readiness
  • reflect on strengths, growth areas, and next-step development goals

Required Learner Practicum Documents

  • practicum agreement form
  • site placement approval record
  • orientation completion acknowledgment
  • hour tracking log
  • weekly reflection entries
  • midpoint feedback form
  • final evaluation form

Practicum Reflection Requirements

Learners should submit structured reflections that address:

  • what was observed
  • how peer support principles were demonstrated
  • what communication or boundary lessons were learned
  • what questions or challenges arose
  • how the experience shaped workforce readiness

Midpoint and Final Evaluation Areas

Evaluation Area Description
Professional Conduct Punctuality, reliability, respectful behavior, and accountability
Communication Listening, clarity, respectful interaction, and peer-appropriate language
Boundary Awareness Professional role limits and ethical conduct
Recovery Orientation Use of hope-centered, person-driven engagement
Cultural Responsiveness Respect for diverse backgrounds and responsive interaction
Navigation Readiness Basic resource awareness and referral support ability
Scope Awareness Recognition of when to consult, refer, or escalate
Overall Readiness General preparedness for peer workforce entry

Completion Requirements

To complete the practicum overlay successfully, the learner must:

  • complete required practicum hours
  • maintain satisfactory professional conduct
  • submit all required reflection and tracking documents
  • receive satisfactory site and faculty evaluation
  • comply with site expectations and confidentiality standards

Practicum Non-Completion

Practicum non-completion may result from:

  • failure to complete required hours
  • unsatisfactory professional conduct
  • confidentiality violation
  • safety-related concerns
  • failure to submit required documentation
  • site removal for policy violation

Liability and Site Compliance Statement

All practicum participation must follow Rhombus University requirements and host site rules. Learners must remain within approved educational scope. Site participation does not authorize the learner to act independently beyond the structured practicum arrangement.
