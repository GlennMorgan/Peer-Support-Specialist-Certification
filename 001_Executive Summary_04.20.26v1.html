Executive Summary
Rhombus University Peer Support Specialist Training Provider Application

Rhombus University is preparing submission to CalMHSA to become an approved provider of the Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist 80-hour core training. The instructional package is substantially complete. Remaining steps involve administrative assembly and hybrid delivery confirmation prior to July submission.

Purpose

Approval authorizes Rhombus University to deliver the required 80-hour training used by individuals pursuing Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist certification for employment in California behavioral health systems.

This establishes Rhombus University as a workforce training provider supporting recovery services, navigation programs, and peer workforce development aligned with the Rhombus service ecosystem.

Hybrid Instructional Delivery Model (Rhombus + ECTLC)

The proposed delivery structure combines live virtual instruction delivered by Rhombus University with structured in-person skills labs hosted at East County Transitional Living Center (ECTLC).

Strategic Value

  • Establishes Rhombus University as a behavioral health workforce training provider
  • Creates an internal peer workforce development pipeline
  • Supports recovery-aligned service expansion initiatives
  • Strengthens alignment with county and community behavioral health systems
  • Positions Rhombus for future specialty training approvals
